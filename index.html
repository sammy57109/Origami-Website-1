Interesting Facts About Camels

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Interesting Facts About Chameleons

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  • Changing Color is an important part of communication among Chameleons.
  • Most chameleons have a prehesile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Interesting Facts About Cicadas

  • Cicadas do not sting or bite, according to the University of Michigan.
  • Cicadas make noise like toy frogs.
  • They’re underground builders

Interesting Facts About Pandas

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Interesting Facts About Pigeons

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowed for their outstanding navigational abilities
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing ability

Interesting Facts About Teddy Bears

  • There is a dedicated magazine to teddy bears. This magazine is called ‘Teddy Bear and Friends’.
  • There are about 2,000 speciality bear making stores in America! This does not count stores like Build-A-Bear.
  • Mohair fur comes from goats. The goat’s hair is shorn and combed before being used to make hand-made bears.
